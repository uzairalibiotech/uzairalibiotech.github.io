About

A bit about my work

Research interest Neurometabolic regulation: the biology connecting metabolic reprogramming and neuronal signalling. In plainer terms: I like figuring out how to turn white fat brown, and how neurons decide when to fire.

I'm a research scientist working at the junction of metabolic and neuro biology. Most of my published work focuses on adipocytes, particularly how they switch between energy storing and energy burning states, and how that connects to metabolic disease. Alongside this, my postdoctoral work on neuromodulation gave me hands on experience with primary neuronal assays, viral gene delivery, and calcium imaging in neurons. My toolkit spans cell culture, plasmid and viral vector gene delivery, gene expression analysis, confocal and calcium imaging, and the statistical and bioinformatic analysis that turns those experiments into a story.

I've worked in international, collaborative research environments across Italy, Sweden, Finland, and Germany, coordinating projects and supervising junior researchers along the way. Since mid 2025 I've been building an independent research and consulting practice: peer reviewing for international journals, giving invited lectures on research careers and international academic pathways, and staying engaged with the wider scientific community through conferences and continued training.

Why I'm starting this journal

After a PhD in Udine, a postdoc chasing neurons and adipocytes across Erlangen, Helsinki, and Stockholm, and now a year into building an independent research and consulting practice, I wanted a single place to point people to: one link for a hiring committee, a collaborator, or a student asking about the path into an international PhD.

I'll use this space for short updates: a conference I've just come back from, a paper that made me rethink an assumption, or a note for early career researchers navigating academia across borders, as I've had to a few times myself. Nothing polished, and not always frequent. Just notes from the bench and beyond.

More field notes coming soon. Check back after the next conference.
